**Transformation Science** is an open research organization dedicated to the scientific study of morphological transformations. It serves as a collaborative space for contributors working on the biological, computational, and engineering principles underlying large-scale anatomical change from human morphology to alternative anatomical configurations, drawing on morphogenesis, metamorphosis, developmental biology, biomechanics, synthetic biology, and related fields. The project supports work across modeling, theory, and simulation, with contributions guided by scientific rigor, reproducibility, and evidence-based reasoning grounded in established scientific knowledge.

## Repository Guidelines

- Keep discussions focused on scientific research of morphological transformations.
- Keep contributions evidence-based, reproducible, and scientifically rigorous.
- Ethics, politics, ideology, and public policy are outside the scope of this repository.
- Resolve scientific disagreements through evidence, reproducible results, sound reasoning, and relevant literature rather than appeals to authority or personal opinion.
- Critique ideas, not people. Assume good faith and remain professional.

Maintainers may redirect or close discussions that fall outside the project's scope.
